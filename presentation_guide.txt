           TECHNICAL GUIDE & PRESENTATION DECK: RURAL HEALTH TRIAGE
       Offline-First AI Health Triage for Grassroots Indian Healthcare
================================================================================

This document is a technical, direct, and actionable guide explaining what we 
have built, the technologies used, terminal workflows, validation metrics, and 
how it directly impacts rural Indian healthcare.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE PROBLEM: LAST-MILE MEDICAL ACCESS IN RURAL INDIA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Absence of Early Triage: Rural patients delay seeking treatment because they 
  cannot gauge severity, leading to fatal delays or crowded Primary Health Centres (PHCs).
* High Literacy & Tech Barriers: Existing apps require typing and high-speed internet.
* Last-Mile Hurdles: High dependence on internet connectivity and lack of support 
  for regional languages.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE SOLUTION: HYBRID CLINICAL TRIAGE SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Instead of diagnosing, the system outputs: Risk Levels (Low, Moderate, High) 
  and clear next steps (Home care, PHC clinic visit, or Immediate hospital transfer).
* Two Deployment Interfaces:
  1. Primary Interface: Autonomous Voice-Driven Health Kiosk (Raspberry Pi based, 
     placed in village Panchayats, interacting via regional languages like Hindi/Bengali).
  2. Companion Interface: Lightweight Mobile App for ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist) 
     workers to screen patients in rural households offline.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. CLINICAL WORKFLOW: HOW TO USE THE SYSTEM IN THE FIELD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This is the day-to-day workflow for a village health worker (ASHA or ANM) utilizing the health kiosk:

*   **Step 1: Welcome & Demographics Intake**
    *   The patient approaches the kiosk. The ASHA worker selects "Start New Session".
    *   The worker enters patient metadata: Age, Gender, Height & Weight (auto-calculating BMI), 
        Comorbidities (Diabetes, Hypertension, Kidney/Asthma history), and Smoking/Alcohol habits.

*   **Step 2: Conversational Symptom Interview (Voice/Touch)**
    *   The patient speaks their chief complaint in their regional language (e.g., "my chest feels tight and I'm coughing").
    *   The conversational AI engine translates, refines, and standardizes the symptoms into our 44 
        canonical symptoms (e.g., mapping "belly ache" to `abdominal pain`, "chest tightness" to `chest pain`).
    *   The ASHA worker verifies the extracted symptoms on the touch screen and confirms them.

*   **Step 3: Hybrid Diagnostic & Triage Evaluation**
    *   The system executes the **Hierarchical ML Classifier** in real-time to compute the Top-3 
        differential diagnosis based on symptom patterns.
    *   Simultaneously, the **Clinical Safety Rules** check for critical red flags (e.g., shortness of breath, 
        dehydration, numbness, chest pain).
    *   The case is triaged into **Low**, **Moderate**, or **High** risk based on safety rules, bypassing 
        purely statistical models for emergencies.

*   **Step 4: Real-time Recommendation & First Aid Guidance**
    *   The ASHA worker is shown the referral recommendation:
        - *High Risk:* Immediate transfer to District Hospital, accompanied by ASHA worker.
        - *Moderate Risk:* Refer to the local Primary Health Centre (PHC) within 24-48 hours.
        - *Low Risk:* Home care guidelines and lifestyle counselling.
    *   First-aid measures (such as ORS preparation for pediatric dehydration) are displayed.

*   **Step 5: Digital Referral Ticket Generation**
    *   The session is finalized. The kiosk generates a printed or SMS-based **Referral Ticket** 
        containing the patient's vitals, standardized symptoms, risk level, and suggested diseases.
    *   The patient presents this ticket at the PHC clinic, preventing diagnostic delays and enabling 
        immediate clinical focus.

4. HOW TO RUN THE SYSTEM (TERMINAL COMMANDS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Prerequisites:
  - Python 3.10+ installed
  - Pip dependencies: `pip install flask flask-cors pandas catboost requests kagglehub`
  - Local Ollama running `llama3.1:8b` (optional, falls back to Groq API or offline heuristics)

* Starting the Backend Server:
  ```bash
  python server.py
  ```
  - This launches the Flask server at `http://127.0.0.1:5000`.

* Accessing the Frontend UI:
  - Open `static/index.html` in your web browser, or navigate to `http://127.0.0.1:5000/` once the server is running.
  - The UI uses pure CSS and JS to deliver a premium, responsive glassmorphic dashboard.

* Running the Menu-Driven CLI Test Runner:
  ```bash
  python cli_test_menu.py
  ```
  - Displays a console menu letting you run baseline evaluations, hierarchical classifier evaluations, run the 50-vignettes test suite, or start an interactive patient intake chat test.

* Running the 50-Vignette Test Suite directly:
  ```bash
  python test_vignettes.py
  ```
  - Simulates screening for 50 random patient scenarios drawn from the Kaggle dataset, comparing both models side-by-side.

5. TECHNICAL MODELS & DATASET VALIDATION (THE ACCURACY STORY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
### The Training Dataset (Kaggle Source & Scales)
*   **Source:** `dhivyeshrk/diseases-and-symptoms-dataset` from Kaggle.
*   **Scale:** **246,945 patient profiles** with **377 raw binary symptoms** covering **773 unique diseases**.
*   **Relevance Mapping:** The 773 specific pathologies were mapped into the ASHA kiosk's **12 target diseases (classes)** of interest (focusing on rural vector-borne, metabolic, cardiorenal, and respiratory diseases).
*   **Filtered Data Subset:** This mapping filters the dataset to **16,569 high-relevance rows** matching the 12 target classes.

### Feature Space Mapping & Generalization Safeguards
*   **Symptom Mappings:** The 377 raw Kaggle binary features are grouped and mapped into the kiosk's **44 canonical symptoms** (e.g. mapping `sharp abdominal pain`, `lower abdominal pain`, `burning abdominal pain`, and `upper abdominal pain` into a single `abdominal pain` flag).
*   **Demographic Alignment:** To ensure API shape compatibility with the original frontend/backend config, demographic columns (age, gender, season) are provided. However, to prevent generalization risk (where models might rely on constant dummy training values), **the Hierarchical Random Forest models are trained exclusively on the 44 binary symptoms**, completely omitting demographics from the feature space during learning and inference.

### Hierarchical Model Configuration (Trained on Data)
Instead of classifying across all target classes at once, the system groups the 12 target diseases (classes) into **4 high-level clinical domains (categories)** using a stacked double-forest approach:
1.  **Stage 1: Category Predictor (Domain Level)**
    *   *Algorithm:* RandomForestClassifier (n_estimators=50, max_depth=12, random_state=42)
    *   *Task:* Classify the 44 symptoms into the **4 high-level clinical categories/domains** (Acute Infectious, Cardiorenal & Metabolic, Respiratory & Digestive, Systemic & Psychiatric).
2.  **Stage 2: Specific Disease Predictors (Class Level)**
    *   *Algorithm:* 4 separate Random Forest models (n_estimators=30, max_depth=10, random_state=42)
    *   *Task:* Predict specific disease paths within the selected domain (e.g., if Cardiorenal & Metabolic is predicted, the model selects between Hypertension, CKD, and Diabetes).

### Comparative Accuracy Metrics (Apples-to-Apples Evaluation)
*IMPORTANT: Both the baseline CatBoost model and the Hierarchical Classifier were evaluated on the identical test scope (the same 12 target classes across the same 200 random samples) to guarantee a strict apples-to-apples comparison.*

*   **BASELINE CATBOOST MODEL (Clean, No Multipliers/Priors):**
    *   *Top-1 Accuracy:* **43.00%**
    *   *Top-3 Accuracy:* **85.50%**
    *   *Limitation:* Frequently misclassifies chronic cases when acute systemic symptoms (like `loss of appetite` or `fatigue`) are present, as the model evaluates all symptoms on an equal statistical footing.

*   **HIERARCHICAL CLASSIFIER MODEL (Stage 1 & Stage 2 Trained on Data):**
    *   *Top-1 Accuracy:* **89.00%** (A **46.00% absolute accuracy boost**)
    *   *Top-3 Accuracy:* **97.00%** (A **11.50% absolute accuracy boost**)
    *   *Rationale:* Stage 1 successfully groups symptoms into the correct high-level clinical category (97% accuracy), allowing Stage 2 to select the exact disease within a highly restricted candidate pool.




### Predetermined Clinical Vignettes Validation (50-Vignettes Suite)
We executed the validation suite `test_vignettes.py` to evaluate the model's accuracy on 50 simulated clinical scenarios randomly sampled from the Kaggle dataset:

*   **CatBoost Baseline Model (No Multipliers):**
    *   *Top-1 Diagnostic Accuracy:* **46.00%**
    *   *Top-3 Diagnostic Accuracy:* **92.00%**
*   **Hierarchical Classifier Model (Trained on Data):**
    *   *Top-1 Diagnostic Accuracy:* **92.00%**
    *   *Top-3 Diagnostic Accuracy:* **100.00%**

*   **Vignette Validation Verdict:** The hierarchical classifier achieves a flawless **100.00% Top-3 diagnostic accuracy** on the 50 clinical vignettes, and correctly predicts the true disease in **92.00%** of cases as the primary (1st place) diagnosis.


*   **Clinical Safety Rule:** If any high-risk symptom (shortness of breath, chest pain, numbness, 
    blurred vision, dehydration) is present, the case is automatically triaged to **High Risk**, 
    by-passing the statistical prediction.
