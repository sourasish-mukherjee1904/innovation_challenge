================================================================================
           TECHNICAL GUIDE & PRESENTATION DECK: RURAL HEALTH TRIAGE
       Offline-First AI Health Triage for Grassroots Indian Healthcare
================================================================================

This document is a technical, direct, and actionable guide explaining what we 
have built, the technologies used, and how it directly impacts rural Indian healthcare.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. THE PROBLEM: LAST-MILE MEDICAL ACCESS IN RURAL INDIA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Absence of Early Triage: Rural patients delay seeking treatment because they 
  cannot gauge severity, leading to fatal delays or crowded Primary Health Centres (PHCs).
* High Literacy & Tech Barriers: Existing apps require typing and high-speed internet.
* Last-Mile Hurdles: High dependence on internet connectivity and lack of support 
  for regional languages.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE SOLUTION: HYBRID CLINICAL TRIAGE SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Instead of diagnosing, the system outputs: Risk Levels (Low, Moderate, High) 
  and clear next steps (Home care, PHC visit, or Immediate hospital transfer).
* Two Deployment Interfaces:
  1. Primary Interface: Autonomous Voice-Driven Health Kiosk (Raspberry Pi based, 
     placed in village Panchayats, interacting via regional languages like Hindi/Bengali).
  2. Companion Interface: Lightweight Mobile App for ASHA (Accredited Social Health Activist) 
     workers to screen patients in rural households offline.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION: WHAT WE HAVE BUILT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
We built a working hybrid prototype combining web interfaces, lightweight local ML, 
and resilient API fallbacks.

* Frontend Client Dashboard:
  - Demographic Onboarding: Gathers age, gender, height/weight (BMI), comorbidities, 
    and lifestyle details (smoking/alcohol status).
  - Triage Chat Room: Interactive, glassmorphic conversational chat representing the 
    clinical interview.
  - Diagnostic Report: Live confidence progress bars, matched symptoms, and suggested treatments.

* Backend Orchestrator (Flask Server):
  - Manages active session states, parses demographics, and computes metrics (BMI/Season).
  - Hosts API endpoints: `/api/start-session`, `/api/chat`, and `/api/finalize`.

* Resilient Dual-LLM Conversational Layer:
  - Primary (Ollama Llama 3.1:8b): Runs locally on the client/edge machine for offline use 
    and complete data privacy.
  - Secondary Fallback (Groq API - Llama 3.3 70B): Instantly takes over if local Ollama 
    is offline, returning responses under 1 second.
  - Last-Resort Heuristic Parser: Runs basic keyword NLP matching if both servers fail, 
    preventing any service downtime.

* Machine Learning Risk Predictor:
  - Powered by CatBoost Classifier (`disease_prediction_model.cbm`) running locally on 
    the backend.
  - Maps extracted symptoms to standard clinical features to compute disease risk 
    likelihood and suggested treatments.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. PILOT METRICS & SUCCESS CRITERIA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Success is measured by direct clinical utility:
* Triage Accuracy: ≥85% agreement with clinical practitioners.
* False-Negative Rate: <5% (High-risk cases must never be misclassified as low-risk).
* System Uptime: ≥95% availability under offline-primary field conditions.
* ASHA Worker Adoption: ≥80% of active health workers completing ≥10 interactions.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. REGULATORY & CLINICAL SAFETY (CDSCO & GOVERNANCE)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
* Clinical Co-Design: Triage logic and ML outputs are designed alongside medical 
  professionals to guarantee interpretability and safety.
* Indian Regulatory Compliance: Built with awareness of CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard 
  Control Organisation) requirements for medical device software in India.
* Research-First Governance: Pilot trials will be governed as research studies rather 
  than open services to ensure strict clinical oversight.
